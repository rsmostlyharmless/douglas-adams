Douglas Adams

Primary phase.

Douglas Adams was born in Cambridge on the 11th March 1952. Shortly after he was born his family moved to East London where Douglas' sister was born. Both Douglas and his sister then moved to Brentwood, Essex with their mother after the divorce of their parents in 1957.

Secondary phase.

In 1959, Douglas Adams would go to prep school and then to main school in Brentwood where he would be known not just for his writing skills but also his height as by the age of 12 he stood at 6ft 5". Adams would then attend St John's College in Cambridge after being awarded an Exhibition in English for writing an essay on religious poetry that discussed The Beatles and William Blake. He went on to graduate in 1974 with a 2:2 in English Literature.

Tertiary phase

After graduating in 1974, Adams moved to London determined to become a writer for TV and radio. Later that year Adams was discovered by Monty Python's Graham Chapman which led the pair to write together for a brief period. In 1977 Adams, along with producer Simon Brett, pitched The Hitchhikers Guide to the Galaxy to BBC Radio 4. The first series aired in 1978 which went on to be adapted into books, tv series, stage shows, a film, and even a video game.

Quandary phase

In 1985, Adams joined a project set up by the World Wide Fund for Nature and The Observer which was to raise awareness for various endangered species around the world. The project started in Madagascar where Adams met with zoologist, Mark Carwardine. With the project being successful, Adams and Carwardine made a radio documentary series call Last Chance to See, which was released in 1989 of which followed a book in 1990. During this time Adams also wrote the books, Dirk Gently's Holistic Detective Agency (1987) and The Long, Dark Tea Time of the Soul (1988). Both these books were later turned into a radio series and also a 2 separate tv shows.

Quintessential phase

After the release of the documentary series Last Chance to See, Adams went on to help with projects such as the Great Ape Project (created in 1993) and the Dian Fossey Gorilla Fund (created in 1978). In 1994 Adams climbed Mount Kilimanjaro while wearing a rhino costume for the charity Save the Rhino International. Adams also had a passion for technology. He brought his first word processor in 1982 which was a Nexu. Shortly after in 1984 the Macintosh was released, of which Adams was the first person in Europe to buy one and was an avid user and endorser thereafter. Adams was also an atheist. Though he actually told people he was a "radical atheist" so this was not confused with agnostic.

Hexagonal phase

Douglas Adams passed away on 11th May 2001 from a heart attack. 2 days prior to his death the asteroid 18610 was named after Hithhickers guide character Auther Dent and then in 2005 the asteroid 25924 was named Douglas Adams. In 2002 The Salmon of Doubt was published containing various writings by Douglas Adams, some of which were previously unpublished. Adams had said that he wanted to write a sixth Hitchhikers Guide book as the ending to Mostly Harmless was quite "bleak". This was later written by Eoin Colfer titled And Another Thing......, which was released on 12th October 2009. The legacy of Douglas Adams still continues to this day through his writings and activism.
